offline geocoding · spatial cluster detection

Put your cases on the map without putting them on the internet.

MESA is a desktop application for public-health GIS. It geocodes addresses and finds spatial clusters entirely on your own machine. No network, no third-party services, no patient data leaving your computer.

01 capabilities

geocode

Addresses, offline

You supply the authoritative address data, Utah's UGRC points or your own locality's, and a bundled tool builds it into a local SQLite index the geocoder matches against. Optional supplemental files add coverage.

analyze

Find the clusters

Local hot- and cold-spot detection with Getis-Ord Gi* and a Bernoulli spatial scan, reported alongside a global Moran's I autocorrelation diagnostic. Monte-Carlo inference, bit-reproducible across every platform.

prepare

Clean and join tables

Opens Cognos MHT, CSV, and Excel exports. Fix columns, join tables on shared keys, and export to CSV, Excel, or spatial formats.

protect

PHI stays yours

No telemetry and no outbound connections during analysis. Protected health information never touches a server you don't control.

serve

A geocoder for ArcGIS Pro

MESA can expose the same offline geocoder as a local ArcGIS GeocodeServer endpoint. Point ArcGIS Pro straight at the URL and batch-geocode your tables against the address data you've loaded, with no ArcGIS Enterprise and no tokens. It binds to localhost by default, so requests never leave the machine.

02 workflow

  1. step 01

    Load your table

    Drag in a Cognos MHT, CSV, or Excel export. MESA parses it, profiles the columns, and tells you what is missing.

    .mht · .csv · .xlsx
  2. step 02

    Geocode offline

    Map the address column and match every row against the authoritative address data you've loaded. Each result carries a precision label.

    authoritative data you supply
  3. step 03

    Detect clusters

    Run Getis-Ord Gi* or a Bernoulli scan with Monte-Carlo inference to surface the hot and cold spots that matter.

    Gi* · Bernoulli scan
  4. step 04

    Export with provenance

    Write results back to CSV, Excel, or a spatial format, precision labels and all, ready for the next tool or a report.

    .csv · .xlsx · spatial
Every coordinate is labeled, because certainty is not accuracy.

centroidZIP or city placeholder, locality only

03 offline

Spatial epidemiology usually means shipping addresses to a geocoding API. MESA doesn't. The address database lives on your disk and the analysis runs in-process, so you can work on an air-gapped machine and still get reproducible results. Every figure on this page, including the scan below, is real MESA output computed entirely offline.
